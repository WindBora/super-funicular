                                                                                                         V. CONCLUSION
                                                                                We have developed an economic and accurate numerical
                                                                            method able to compute 2-D models of electrically large
                                                                            multireflector antennas of arbitrary shape in seconds, with
                                                                            desired accuracy. This method, MDS, works with classical
                                                                            electric-field SIEs for the surface currents, however, unlike
                                                                            conventional MoM versions, has guaranteed and controlled
Fig. 17. NRPs for single and double-source PACO antennas with d = c = 30   convergence. It differs also from the analytical regularization
and kb = 9:2; f = 0:5d; x = y = 0.                                          methods [15], [16] which use the inversion of the static parts
                                                                            of these SIE. Unlike them, MDS exploits direct discretization
                                                                            of SIE, however in specific manner based on the Nystrom-type
                                                                            interpolation schemes of the highest accuracy that take account
                                                                            of the edge behavior of unknown function and the singular
                                                                            character of the kernel. So far, MDS has not been applied to
                                                                            the reflector analysis and, in our opinion, it has advantage over
                                                                            the other methods as either more accurate or more flexible and
                                                                            easier in implementation. MDS enables one to study near and
                                                                            far field patterns, surface currents, and also directivity and gain
                                                                            for various reflectors, feed locations, etc.
                                                                                In this paper, we have presented MDS analysis for the case of
                                                                               -polarization; the emphasis lies with the analysis of large one
                                                                            and two-reflector antennas that would be prohibited with a MoM
                                                                            subdomain discretization. As an example, we have performed
